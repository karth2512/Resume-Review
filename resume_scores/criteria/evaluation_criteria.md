# Evaluation Criteria: Senior Software Engineer - Full Stack

This document contains the structured evaluation criteria extracted from the job description.

## Required Technical Skills

### JavaScript proficiency

**Weight:** Critical  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for JavaScript explicitly mentioned in skills section, project descriptions showing JavaScript implementation, or work experience with JavaScript-based applications. Check for depth of experience through complexity of projects described.

---

### TypeScript proficiency

**Weight:** Critical  
**Evaluation Guide:** Check for TypeScript in skills list, projects built with TypeScript, or work experience mentioning TypeScript. Look for understanding of type systems and modern TypeScript features in project descriptions.

---

### Modern web frameworks (React, Vue, or Angular)

**Weight:** Critical  
**Evaluation Guide:** Identify specific framework experience (React, Vue, or Angular) in skills section or project work. Assess depth through descriptions of components built, state management, or framework-specific patterns used.

---

### Backend development (Node.js, Python, or Java)

**Weight:** Critical  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for at least one of these backend technologies mentioned with practical application. Verify through API development, server-side logic implementation, or backend service creation in work history.

---

### RESTful APIs understanding

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Check for explicit mention of REST API design/development, API integration work, or descriptions of building/consuming RESTful services. Look for understanding of HTTP methods, status codes, and REST principles.

---

### Microservices architecture experience

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for projects involving microservices design, distributed systems work, or mentions of service-oriented architecture. Assess understanding through descriptions of service decomposition or inter-service communication.

---

### SQL databases (PostgreSQL or similar)

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Check for PostgreSQL, MySQL, SQL Server, or other SQL database experience. Look for database design, query optimization, or complex SQL operations in project descriptions.

---

### NoSQL databases (MongoDB or similar)

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Identify MongoDB, Cassandra, DynamoDB, or other NoSQL database experience. Look for schema design, document modeling, or NoSQL-specific operations mentioned in work history.

---

### Git version control

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Check for Git mentioned in skills or experience with Git workflows (branching, merging, pull requests). Look for GitHub/GitLab profiles or collaborative development experience.

---

### CI/CD pipelines experience

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for mentions of Jenkins, GitLab CI, GitHub Actions, CircleCI, or similar tools. Verify through descriptions of automated testing, deployment pipelines, or release processes.

---

### Cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, or GCP)

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Identify specific cloud platform experience. Look for cloud service usage (EC2, S3, Lambda, Azure Functions, etc.), cloud architecture design, or cloud deployments in work experience.

---

### Containerization (Docker, Kubernetes)

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Check for Docker and/or Kubernetes experience. Look for container creation, orchestration, or containerized application deployment in project descriptions.

---

## Preferred Technical Skills

### Agile/Scrum methodologies

**Weight:** Medium  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for mentions of Agile, Scrum, Kanban, or sprint-based development in work experience. Check for roles like Scrum team member, sprint planning participation, or Agile ceremonies.

---

### DevOps practices

**Weight:** Medium  
**Evaluation Guide:** Check for DevOps tools and practices such as infrastructure as code, monitoring, logging, automation scripts, or DevOps culture participation. Look for tools like Terraform, Ansible, or monitoring solutions.

---

### Open-source contributions

**Weight:** Low  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for GitHub profile links, mentions of open-source projects contributed to, or maintained open-source libraries. Check for pull requests, issues filed, or project maintainer roles.

---

## Experience Requirements

### 5+ years of professional software development experience

**Weight:** Critical  
**Evaluation Guide:** Calculate total years of relevant professional experience from work history dates. Count only professional roles involving software development, excluding internships unless explicitly relevant. Must meet or exceed 5 years.

---

### Experience mentoring junior developers

**Weight:** Medium  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for explicit mentions of mentoring, coaching, or training junior developers. Check for responsibilities including code review, onboarding, or technical guidance in previous roles.

---

### Proven track record of delivering high-quality software solutions

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Assess through descriptions of completed projects, successful product launches, performance improvements, or measurable outcomes. Look for indicators of quality such as testing practices, code standards, or production stability.

---

## Education Requirements

### Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related field

**Weight:** Medium  
**Evaluation Guide:** Check education section for Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Software Engineering, Information Technology, or closely related technical fields. Note: Listed as 'preferred' so equivalent experience may substitute.

---

## Soft Skills

### Strong problem-solving skills

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for descriptions of complex technical challenges solved, troubleshooting experience, or innovative solutions implemented. Assess through examples of debugging, optimization, or architectural problem-solving.

---

### Attention to detail

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Evaluate through mentions of testing practices, code quality focus, bug-free deliveries, or meticulous documentation. Look for quality assurance involvement or precision in technical descriptions.

---

### Strong communication skills

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for evidence of cross-functional collaboration, technical documentation, presentations, or stakeholder communication. Check for clear, well-written resume as initial indicator.

---

### Teamwork skills

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Assess through descriptions of collaborative projects, cross-functional team participation, pair programming, or team-based achievements. Look for 'we' vs 'I' language in accomplishments.

---

### Code review capabilities

**Weight:** Medium  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for explicit mentions of conducting code reviews, peer reviews, or ensuring code quality standards. May be implied through senior-level positions or mentoring experience.

---

## Domain Knowledge

### Understanding of scalable web application architecture

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for experience with high-traffic applications, scaling challenges addressed, performance optimization, or distributed systems. Check for mentions of load balancing, caching, or horizontal scaling.

---

### Production troubleshooting and issue resolution

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Check for on-call experience, incident response, debugging production issues, or post-mortem analysis. Look for monitoring, logging, or observability tools mentioned.

---

### Technical architecture decision-making

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for senior or lead roles involving architecture design, technology selection, or system design decisions. Check for mentions of technical proposals, design documents, or architecture reviews.

---

## Other Requirements

### Ability to write clean, maintainable, well-tested code

**Weight:** Critical  
**Evaluation Guide:** Assess through mentions of unit testing, integration testing, test-driven development (TDD), code quality practices, or testing frameworks. Look for emphasis on maintainability, documentation, or clean code principles.

---

### Collaboration with cross-functional teams

**Weight:** High  
**Evaluation Guide:** Look for experience working with product managers, designers, QA, or other non-engineering stakeholders. Check for descriptions of feature delivery requiring coordination across teams.

---
